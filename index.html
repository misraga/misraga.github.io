Hi there!

I am a PhD candidate in the Mechanical and Aerospace Engineering Department at Rutgers, The State University of New Jersey, where I am working with Prof. Xiaoli Bai .

In general, I work on the intersection of Aerospace Control, Robotics, and Convex Optimization. Some of the buzzwords that my research interests relate to include: Stochastic Optimization, Polynomial Optimization, Space Systems, Model Predictive Control , Flight Dynamics and Control, Optimal Control, and Robot Motion Planning.

Currently, my Ph.D. research centers around tractable convex optimization based planning and control methods for aerospace systems with applications in space robotic system enabled debris mitigation and carrier based aircraft landing control.

Before coming to Rutgers, I received an MS in Aerospace Engineering from New Mexico State University where I was a graduate research assistant at the Spacecraft Guidance, Navigation, and Controls Lab. My Master's thesis advisor was Prof. Amit Sanyal. My MS thesis research focused on coupled orbit-attitude spacecraft dynamics in the proximity of asteroids. I also looked into the nonlinear controllability of underactuated spacecraft in such enviornments using differential geometry tools.

I was also fortunate to complete my bachelor thesis at the French Space Agency (CNES), Toulouse, France. My bachelor thesis supervisor was Jean-Yves Prado and I worked on asteroid hazard mitigation using Yarkovsky effect reduction.
